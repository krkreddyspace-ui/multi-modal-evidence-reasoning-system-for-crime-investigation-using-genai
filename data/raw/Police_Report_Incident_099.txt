CENTRAL CITY POLICE DEPARTMENT - INCIDENT REPORT
REPORT ID: CCPD-2026-0099-HR
CASE TYPE: VEHICULAR ASSAULT / HIT-AND-RUN
REPORTING OFFICER: SGT. MARCUS REED
DATE: MARCH 26, 2026

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. INCIDENT SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On March 25, 2026, at approximately 23:30 hours, emergency services responded 
to a 911 call regarding a pedestrian struck at the intersection of 5th and Main. 
The victim, identified as Leo Banks (24), was found unconscious with severe 
lower-body trauma. Witnesses described a white SUV fleeing the scene at high 
speed immediately after the impact.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. SUSPECT STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Officer David Miller (Badge #4412) was identified as a Person of Interest 
based on vehicle type. Officer Miller voluntarily provided a statement on 
March 26 at 02:00 hours.

Miller states: "I was nowhere near 5th and Main at 11:30 PM. I had just finished 
my shift and drove straight to the Bean & Brew coffee shop on 12th Street to 
decompress. I was there for about twenty minutes. I have the receipt to prove 
it."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. PHYSICAL ALIBI EVIDENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Evidence Item #1A: A paper receipt from 'Bean & Brew' Coffee House.
- Transaction Time: 23:35:12 (11:35 PM)
- Location: 12th Street Branch (approx. 5 miles from the crime scene)
- Purchase: 1x Large Black Coffee.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PRELIMINARY VEHICLE INSPECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Officer Miller's vehicle (White 2024 Ford Explorer) was visually inspected. 
The officer claims a small dent on the front bumper was caused by a minor 
collision with a parking garage pillar two days prior. No blood was visible 
to the naked eye. The vehicle has been impounded for forensic analysis.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. VICTIM STATUS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Victim Leo Banks remains in critical condition. Personal effects recovered 
at the scene include a heavily damaged blue denim jacket. Frictional transfer 
analysis is pending.
